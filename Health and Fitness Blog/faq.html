Frequently Asked Questions

Your health questions answered.

How much exercise do I need per week?

Adults should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise (e.g. brisk walking) or 75 minutes of vigorous activity (e.g. running), plus strength training 2+ days per week.

What counts as moderate vs. vigorous activity?

Moderate intensity: breathing faster but can still talk (e.g. brisk walking, light cycling).

Vigorous intensity: hard to speak more than a few words (e.g. running, fast swimming).

Do I need to exercise every day?

No. Focus on total weekly activity. Whether spread throughout the week or done in weekend "warrior" style sessions, meeting weekly targets yields benefits.

How often should I include strength training?

At least two days per week, working all major muscle groups: legs, hips, back, chest, shoulders, arms, and abdomen.

What should I eat to support training?

Eat a balanced diet with sufficient protein from animal or plant sources. Tailor intake to your activity level, age, and fitness goals.
