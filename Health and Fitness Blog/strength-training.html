Core Guidelines

  • Train all major muscle groups **at least twice per week**, with 8–15 reps per set to muscle fatigue :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.
  • Start light and focus on **proper form**, using control and full range of motion before adding weight :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.
  • Gradually increase workload over time through **progressive overload** by raising weight, reps, or number of sets :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.
  • Incorporate compound movements like squats, push-ups, rows, deadlifts, and planks to engage multiple muscle groups :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.
  • Always warm up before exercise and cool down afterwards—form, tempo, and breathing are essential to training safely :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

Beginner Sample Routine (2× per Week)

Squats

3 sets of 8–12 reps: focus on depth and steady tempo.

Push‑ups or Bench Press

3 sets of 8–12 reps: controlled movement, full range.

Deadlifts or Hip Hinges

3 sets of 8–12 reps: hinge from hips with back neutral.

Extra Tips for Success

  • Train core and unilateral movements like planks and single-leg exercises to build balance and prevent imbalances :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.
  • Body-weight, free weights, resistance bands, or machines are all effective—choose what fits your access and comfort :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.
  • Expect to see noticeable improvements within 4–6 weeks—with consistency over 8–12 weeks for meaningful strength gains :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.
  • Pair training with adequate nutrition—especially quality protein intake at ~0.8g per kg body weight—to support strength and recovery :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.
